IECC's 2030 base-case levelized cost of steel (LCOS) estimates BF-BOF at ~US$536/t and H₂-DRI/EAF at
~US$562/t—a ~US$26/t premium in nominal US$ terms.

Cost parity when FX + imported‑input exposure are included

BF‑BOF steel is structurally exposed to USD‑priced imported coking coal. IECC adjusts BF‑BOF costs for coal inflation and INR depreciation; even under conservative "half‑historical" adjustment rates, BF‑BOF rises (e.g., to ~US$588/t in the 2030 comparison), shifting H₂‑DRI/EAF from a small premium to parity/advantage under the same 2030 technology assumptions.
